About Digital Culture Schools

OUR VISION

  • In Digital Culture Schools, we believe in the strength of the mind and the need to develop it by providing an educational environment that promotes creativity, critical thinking, and cooperation

School Sections

National Section

Digital Culture schools for both boys and girls adopts the Ministry of Education’s curriculums in the national stream from grades 1-12. In addition to that, Digital culture Schools also adopts an advanced curriculum in English Language that is compatible with the international Standards and the progress of students in English is assessed using MAP tests. Moreover, Digital culture Schools has added some Arabic language enrichment content, such as free reading and creative writing. Digital culture Schools also applies the 21th century skills rubrics, as these are common skills that will be required in the future labour market.

International Section

In the international stream, Digital Culture Schools for boys and girls adopts the international schools’ curriculums in national subjects (Arabic Language, Islamic Education, Social Studies) from grades 1 through 12. Additionally, Digital culture Schools also adopts the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) and English Language and Mathematic curriculums according to the Common Core State Standards CCSS. Digital culture Schools also applies the 21th century skills rubrics as these are common skills that will be required in the future labour market. The international stream will be accredited by Cognia.

Special needs section

Digital Culture Schools have a section dedicated to special education students, which apply the highest quality standards for this category, who are served and taught according to an approved international methodology that suits individual cases and works to integrate them into the surrounding community as the schools provide services (behavior modification - speech and communication sessions - occupational therapy - sensory integration This is for students (intellectual education - behavioral disorders and autism - learning difficulties - sensory and physical disabilities - hyperactivity and distraction - language and speech disorders - multiple disabilities - hearing impairment - visual impairment).
